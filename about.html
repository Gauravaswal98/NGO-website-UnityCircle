Who We Are

A Collective Network for Interfaith Harmony

"We are a collective platform comprising a network of citizens committed to interfaith harmony, including faith leaders, activists, scholars, journalists, and other conscientious voices."

Our Philosophy

UnityCircle seeks to provide a space for interfaith engagement grounded in the principles of mutual respect and dialogue. We believe that by centering the voices of conscientious citizens, we can dismantle prejudice and build a foundation of social solidarity.

Our network bridges the gap between traditional religious institutions and modern civic activism, creating a unique synergy for peace-building.

Vision

To realize a world where diverse spiritual traditions serve as a unified force for the common good, ensuring every citizen lives in a society free from religious discrimination and communal strife.

Mission

To empower interfaith networks through grassroots advocacy, transparent journalism, and scholarly research that highlights shared human values and promotes active peace-building.

Rationale

In an era of rising polarization, individual voices of reason are often drowned out. By organizing as a collective, we amplify the "conscientious majority" to counter hate and promote rational dialogue.

Background & Ongoing Work

The Genesis

UnityCircle began as an informal digital safe-space where journalists and faith leaders shared insights on local conflict resolution during the mid-2010s.

Est. 2016

Current Advocacy

Currently, our network conducts "Harmony Workshops" across 12 cities, engaging over 5,000 students annually in inter-religious literacy and empathy training.

Ongoing Phase

Digital Documentation

We are building an open-source archive of "Solidarity Stories," documenting thousands of instances where communities stood together despite external pressure.
